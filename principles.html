Principles
  • by Jon McMillan

Be curious. Never stop learning. Never stop exploring. Seek to become a Master of your craft. Never stop working to learn more about your craft and complementary crafts.

Be creative. Think outside the box, see things through a variety of views and express yourself. Value creative flexibility and give teams room to experiment with new ideas.

Be patient. There is always a better way and our challenge is to find it, operationalize it and work with our teams to achieve it. This often requires time.

Be fair. Fairness is the baseline for all relationships. Being fair helps establish other values, like trust and confidence.

Be logical. Success requires steps. Itâ€™s important to plan logical steps to get there. Research, digest data, identify gaps and develop workable solutions to close or eliminate those gaps.

Be empathetic. Listen more. Ask questions to understand other points of view. Truly seek to understand the context others are coming from.

Be accessible. Talk and laugh freely with your team. Communicate openly and effectively. Be present in the moment.

Push boundaries. Ask why. Push against norms. Read more. Travel more. Set bigger goals.

Make alone time for yourself. Spend time alone to reflect, meditate and slow life down.

Find balance. Achieving balance is the key for long-term success and happiness. Balance is different for everyone. Find yours.
